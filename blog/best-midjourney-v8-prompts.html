Tips for Modifying These Prompts

These prompts are starting points. Here's how to make them your own:

  1. Change the subject: Keep the style and technical parameters, but swap out the main subject
  2. Adjust stylize (--s): Lower values (0-100) give more literal results; higher values (500-1000) add more artistic flair
  3. Add --hd: Append --hd to any prompt for native 2K resolution output
  4. Experiment with chaos (--c): Add --c 30 for more variety between the four generated images
  5. Use negative prompts: Add --no [unwanted elements] to exclude things you don't want
  6. Mix categories: Combine a portrait style with a fantasy setting, or a food prompt with sci-fi elements

What Makes a Great V8 Prompt?

After testing hundreds of prompts with V8, we've identified the key ingredients:

  • Specificity: V8 handles detailed descriptions much better than vague ones. "A cat" produces mediocre results; the prompts above show the level of detail V8 thrives on.
  • Layered Structure: Subject → Environment → Style → Lighting → Technical. This hierarchy helps V8 prioritize elements.
  • Photography References: Terms like "shot on Hasselblad," "shallow depth of field," and "golden hour" activate V8's photorealistic capabilities.
  • Artistic References: Mentioning specific artists, art movements, or visual media helps V8 understand the aesthetic you're targeting.
  • Mood Words: Words like "ethereal," "dramatic," "intimate," "epic" significantly influence the emotional tone of the output.
